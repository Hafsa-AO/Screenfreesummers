5 Kid-Friendly Museums to Visit This Summer

London is bursting with museums that entertain and educate young minds. Whether you’re after interactive exhibits, storytelling adventures, or just somewhere cool to spend a summer day — these museums deliver.

1. Discover Story Centre (Stratford)

Step into an immersive world of stories where kids can play, imagine, and explore. The Discover Story Centre offers storytelling sessions, a magical story garden, and book-themed installations.

Tip: Entry is just £3–£9 depending on the session — booking online is recommended.

2. Science Museum

From the Wonderlab to hands-on galleries, the Science Museum is packed with interactive learning. Children can build, play, and explore exhibits across space, engineering, and climate.

3. Young V&A (Bethnal Green)

Newly redesigned for creative kids! Young V&A offers play spaces, design studios, and storytelling corners perfect for under-12s. It's free and full of inspiration.

4. Museum of London Docklands

This museum brings London’s history to life with pirate-themed play, Victorian London re-creations, and free family workshops running throughout summer.

5. Horniman Museum & Gardens

With a natural history gallery, musical instruments from around the world, and a butterfly house, Horniman is a delight. Plus, the outdoor gardens and splash fountain are a summer hit.

Explore More: Many museums host special events or free workshops during holidays. Always check the official websites for current programmes.

For families seeking budget-friendly, enriching activities, these museums offer the perfect mix of play and learning — right in the heart of London.
