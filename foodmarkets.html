Family-Friendly & Diverse Food Markets in London

Published July 2025

These lively markets offer global street food, cultural flavours, and open-air fun that kids love. Perfect for casual family outings where everyone can choose something delicious.

Southbank Centre Food Market

Set behind the Royal Festival Hall by the Thames, this weekend market brings together a colourful array of affordable street food—from Ethiopian injera to Korean-inspired burritos and Vietnamese comfort bowls from My Kids Are Hungry. Portions are generous and many dishes come in children’s-sized servings. Families often pair food with riverside walks or playtime in Jubilee Gardens.

Borough Market

This well-loved foodie destination blends affordable international snacks and produce with artisan treats. Try fruity juices, desserts or mini platters perfect for kids. While gourmet, it's easy to keep costs down with small tastings or family sharing.

Camden Market & Hawley Wharf Food Court

Camden Market and riverside Hawley Wharf feature family-friendly stalls serving kid-pleasing foods like casual BBQ, bao or pizza slices. Weekly Kids Summer Fun Wednesdays (July to August) include Lego workshops, craft zones, face-painting and performance—all free to join.

Edgeware Road Halal Food Market

A fresh newcomer to the foodie scene, this halal market offers Middle Eastern and South Asian flavours in a family-friendly setting. Seek out vegan sweets, kids-platter options, and communal seating. While still new, it’s being praised for its inclusive, affordable vibe.

Great British Food Festival – Wimbledon Park

Coming to London for the first time in summer 2025, this festival includes free kids’ cookery demos, foraging walks, street food vendors and a mini zoo. Though festival tickets apply, entry to kids’ cookery and craft areas is free, making it a fun value-packed day out.

Why Families Love These Markets

  • Casual, outdoor spaces where children can explore and wander
  • Diverse tastes available in small or sharable portions
  • Often paired with free creative activities like face-painting or craft zones

Whether it's riverside tacos at Southbank, mini burgers in Camden, or a weekend festival in Wimbledon Park, these markets offer flavour, fun, and family friendly energy for a screen free summer day out.
